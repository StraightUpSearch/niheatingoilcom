<p>The <b>BT postcode area</b>, also known as the <b>Belfast postcode area</b>,<sup id="cite_ref-Guide_2-0" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guide-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> covers all of <a href="/wiki/Northern_Ireland" title="Northern Ireland">Northern Ireland</a> and was the last part of the <a href="/wiki/United_Kingdom" title="United Kingdom">United Kingdom</a> to be coded, between 1970 and 1974.<sup class="noprint Inline-Template Template-Fact" style="white-space:nowrap;">&#91;<i><a href="/wiki/Wikipedia:Citation_needed" title="Wikipedia:Citation needed"><span title="This claim needs references to reliable sources. (June 2009)">citation needed</span></a></i>&#93;</sup> This area is a group of 82 postcode districts in Northern Ireland, within 44 post towns and around 47,227 live postcodes.
</p><p>With a population of over 1.8 million people, BT is the second most populous <a href="/wiki/List_of_postcode_areas_in_the_United_Kingdom" title="List of postcode areas in the United Kingdom">UK postcode area</a>, after the <a href="/wiki/B_postcode_area" title="B postcode area">B postcode area</a> (Birmingham, 1.9 million). It is the only UK postcode area that has land borders with non-UK postcode areas and is also the only UK postcode area that borders the <a href="/wiki/European_Union" title="European Union">European Union</a>. BT borders nine <a href="/wiki/List_of_Eircode_routing_areas_in_Ireland" title="List of Eircode routing areas in Ireland">Eircode</a> routing areas in the neighbouring <a href="/wiki/Republic_of_Ireland" title="Republic of Ireland">Republic of Ireland</a>: F94, F93, F91, N41, H23, H18, H14, A91, and A75.
</p><p>Mail for the BT postcode area is processed at the Northern Ireland Mail Centre in <a href="/wiki/Newtownabbey" title="Newtownabbey">Newtownabbey</a>.
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading2"><h2 id="Coverage">Coverage</h2><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BT_postcode_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=1" title="Edit section: Coverage"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Belfast">Belfast</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BT_postcode_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=2" title="Edit section: Belfast"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>Prior to the introduction of postcodes, Belfast had already been divided into numbered postal districts, for example, Belfast 12.<sup id="cite_ref-3" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-3"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>3<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup> Today, the Belfast <a href="/wiki/Post_town" title="Post town">post town</a> covers postcode districts BT1 to BT17 and part of BT29. In common with all addresses in Northern Ireland, Belfast postcodes start with the letters <i>BT</i>, a <a href="/wiki/Mnemonic" title="Mnemonic">mnemonic</a> of the capital city's name. The <a href="/wiki/Belfast_City_Centre" title="Belfast City Centre">commercial centre</a> of the city is designated <i>BT1</i> and the surrounding districts form a roughly sequential clockwise ring around the city. The postcode of the Royal Mail offices at Tomb Street in Belfast is BT1 1AA.
</p>

<div class="mw-heading mw-heading3"><h3 id="Rest_of_Northern_Ireland">Rest of Northern Ireland</h3><span class="mw-editsection"><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">[</span><a href="/w/index.php?title=BT_postcode_area&amp;action=edit&amp;section=3" title="Edit section: Rest of Northern Ireland"><span>edit</span></a><span class="mw-editsection-bracket">]</span></span></div>
<p>The remainder of Northern Ireland is divided into 43 other post towns which are further divided into postcode districts BT18–BT49, BT51–BT57, BT58 (<a href="/wiki/Non-geographic_postcodes" class="mw-redirect" title="Non-geographic postcodes">non-geographic</a> postcode district reassigned from Belfast to Newtownabbey post town), BT60–BT71, BT74–BT82 and BT92–BT94. Certain addresses in BT29 located at Belfast International Airport (a total of 31 delivery points) are in the post town of BELFAST despite being geographically under the post town of CRUMLIN, which has the remaining 7,206 delivery points in that postcode area.<sup id="cite_ref-Guide_2-1" class="reference"><a href="#cite_note-Guide-2"><span class="cite-bracket">&#91;</span>2<span class="cite-bracket">&#93;</span></a></sup>
